About me

I am an evolutionary developmental biologist, interested in how genomic evolution impacts developmental processes that contribute to morphological diversity. I use big-data approaches with bioinformatics to find insights and test them in a range of in vivo systems.

I completed my honours degree in biology at Oxford Brookes University in 2013. I received my PhD from Oxford Brookes University in 2018, researching the evolution of arthropod genomes and morphology with Alistair McGregor. I then moved to University College London for postdoctoral research with Max Telford, conducting single-cell analyses in a variety of lophotrochozoans species, and deep metazoan phylogenetics. In 2020 I went to Durham University as a postdoctoral researcher, investigating whole genome duplications in chelicerates and single-cell analysis of spider development.

From 2021 to 2023 I was also an Associate Lecturer at Oxford Brookes University teaching undergraduates single-cell sequencing theory and bioinformatic practicals.

You can find out about my research projects and publications, and feel free to contact me.
