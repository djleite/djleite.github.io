I am an evolutional and developmental biologist, interested in how genomic evolution impacts developmental processes that contribute to morphological diversity.

I completed my honours degree in biology at Oxford Brookes University in 2013. I received my PhD from Oxford Brookes University in 2018, researching genome and morphology evolution in arthropods with Alistair McGregor. I then moved to University College London for postdoctoral research with Max Telford, conducting single-cell research in a variety of lophotrochozoans species, and deep metazoan phylogenetics.

In 2020 I moved to Durham University as a postdoctoral researcher, investigating whole genome duplications in chelicerates and single-cell analysis of spider development. I also was an Associate Lecturer at Oxford Brookes University from 2021 - 2013, teaching single-cell sequencing.

You can find out about my research projects and publications. Also, feel free to contact me.
